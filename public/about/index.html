About

I’m Peter (Pétur). I hold a master’s degree in physics and am currently working towards a PhD at the University of Cambridge.

I study the fermiology and exotic quantum phases of quantum materials. My research interests are emergent quantum phenomena, unconventional superconductivity, topological magnetism, and energy technologies.

I did my undergraduate and master’s course at Durham University where I graduated with a first-class degree. Work I did for my master’s thesis led to new insights in magnetic interactions in cubic-chiral materials.

Moody, S., Nielsen, P., Wilson, M. N., et al. Experimental evidence of a change of exchange anisotropy sign with temperature in Zn-substituted Cu2OSeO3. Phys. Rev. Research 3, 043149 (2021). doi: 10.1103/PhysRevResearch.3.043149
